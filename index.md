## Planetary Mineral Physics and Geophysics

I am a **NERC Independent Research Fellow** and mineral physicist at the **University of Leeds**, working on the thermochemical evolution of planetary cores and magma oceans.

My research combines **molecular dynamics simulations**, **statistical thermodynamics**, and **geodynamical models** to understand how rocky planets transition from molten states to structured interiors capable of sustaining long‑lived magnetic fields.

I am based in the **Leeds Centre for Planetary Cores** and collaborate widely across mineral physics, geodynamics, and planetary science.

**Current roles and awards**
- 🌍 NERC Independent Research Fellow (2024– )
- 🏅 Doornbos Memorial Awardee (SEDI)
- 💎 Chair, Mineral Physics Group (Mineralogical Society of the UK & Ireland)

---

### Research focus

- Inner‑core nucleation, slurry layers, and core stratification  
- Molecular dynamics of liquid and supercooled planetary materials  
- Thermodynamics of magma oceans and early planetary evolution  
- Magnetic field generation and planetary habitability

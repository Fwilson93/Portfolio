## Opportunities

I am interested in working with **motivated PhD students and early‑career researchers** who are excited by fundamental problems in planetary interiors, mineral physics, and geodynamics.

My group focuses on how microscopic physical processes — freezing, nucleation, and chemical segregation — shape planetary‑scale evolution.

---

## PhD students

I regularly supervise **PhD projects** at the University of Leeds. Suitable backgrounds include:
- Physics, geophysics, geology, or materials science  
- Computational science or applied mathematics  

Projects may involve:
- Molecular dynamics simulations of planetary materials  
- Thermodynamic modelling of melting and freezing  
- Coupling mineral physics to core or dynamo models  

Prospective students should feel free to contact me to discuss potential topics and funding routes.

---

## Postdoctoral researchers

I am keen to work with postdoctoral researchers interested in:
- Core formation and inner‑core evolution  
- Non‑equilibrium thermodynamics of planetary interiors  
- High‑pressure computation and theory  

I am happy to support applications for independent fellowships and external funding schemes.

---

## Collaborative projects

I welcome collaborations across mineral physics, geodynamics, planetary science, and applied physics — particularly projects that bridge **microscopic physics and planetary observables**.
